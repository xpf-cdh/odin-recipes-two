Rib Roast Recipe

Traditional Holiday Standing Rib Roast

A standing rib roast, also known as prime rib, is a cut of beef from the primal rib, one of the primal cuts of beef. While the entire rib section comprises ribs six through 12, a standing rib roast may contain anywhere from two to seven ribs.

Standing Rib Roast is considered by many to be the best roast beef in the world. Also known as Prime Rib, it’s a beef cut that’s incredibly succulent with superior taste. This recipe uses a safe, simple but highly effective roasting method so the beef is blushing pink all the way through.

Ingredients

  • Rib Roast with at least three ribs and a good amount of fat
  • Kosher Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Olive Oil

How to make a Rib Roast

  1. Remove the roast from the refrigerator and let it get to room temperature
  2. Coat the roast in a light layer of olive oil
  3. Generously coat the roast with kosher salt and crushed black pepper
  4. Place roast in an unheated oven at 250 degrees farenheit
  5. Use a probe thermometer and set it 118 degrees farenheit which will take about three hours
  6. Once the roast reachs 118 degrees remove it from the oven and let it rest for thirty minutes
  7. While the roast is settling heat the oven to 450 degrees farenheit
  8. After thirty minutes have passed and your oven is preheated to 450 degrees we will put the roast back in the oven for ten minutes
  9. Remove the roast and let it settle for ten minutes before it is served

Enjoy!!
